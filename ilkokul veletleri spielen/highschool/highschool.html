Electromagnetic Spectrum and Light Reflection

The electromagnetic spectrum includes different wavelengths of light. The light we can see with our eyes is only a small part of the spectrum. The PACE satellite analyzes the color of water by measuring the light reflected from the ocean surface. Questions: What is the electromagnetic spectrum, and which part does visible light cover? Which colors penetrate deeper into the ocean surface, and why? Why is it important to analyze the color of the ocean?

Photosynthesis and Phytoplankton Activity

Photosynthesis is a process used by plants and phytoplankton to produce energy. The PACE satellite evaluates the activity of phytoplankton by measuring the chlorophyll concentration in the ocean.
Questions:
What components are used in the process of photosynthesis?
Why are phytoplankton so important in the marine ecosystem?
What kind of information does chlorophyll concentration provide about phytoplankton activities?

Chlorophyll and Biological Productivity

Chlorophyll is the green pigment used by phytoplankton. The chlorophyll concentration in the ocean is an indicator of biological productivity.
Questions:
What is chlorophyll, and what is its function?
Why is it important to measure chlorophyll concentration?

Carbon Cycle and Ocean-Atmosphere Interaction

The carbon cycle is the circulation of carbon dioxide between the atmosphere, oceans, soil, and plants. The PACE satellite examines the role of oceans in the carbon cycle.
Questions:
What is the carbon cycle, and what role do oceans play in this cycle?
How do phytoplankton contribute to the carbon cycle?

Ocean Ecosystems and Biological Diversity

Ocean ecosystems are complex structures that sustain marine life. The PACE satellite monitors biological diversity in the ocean.
Questions:
What is the role of phytoplankton in ocean ecosystems?
